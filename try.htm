Andhra cuisine


The cuisine from the South Eastern coast of India is a flavourful blend of cuisines from thwo different regionsâ€”Coastal Andhra and Rayalseema. Also known as the 'Rice Bowl of India', this state is the largest producer of rice and red chillies, two ingredients generously used in Andhrite kitchens. Andhra Pradesh is home to a lot of delicious snacks and breakfast items such as Idli, Upma, Vada, Dosa, Pesarattu, and Atukulu.
Scrumptious cooking options such as Pulusu, Charu, Rasam, Pachadi, Kura make a delicious Andhra meal.Generous use of Chili and Tamarind make the dishes tangy and hot. Bajra roti forms an indispensable part of the Andhra 'Annam' (daily Andhra meal). Pickles and chutneys are a must on this elaborate luncheon. The Andhrites are sweet lovers too. The popular sugary wonders include Laddu, Kakinada Khaja, Ariselu, Gavvalu, Payasam, etc.
Andhra Pradesh produces fiery Andhra cuisine which is largely vegetarian yet has a huge range of seafood in its coastal areas. The majority of a diverse variety of dishes are vegetable- or lentil-based.
